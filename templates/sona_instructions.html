Gun Violence Headline Emotion Detection

Hello, thanks for coming to this survey! The survey is conducted by the Artificial Intelligence and Emerging Media (AIEM) research group at Boston University.

The purpose of this study is to understand how people feel when they come across certain news headlines and images. We are asking you to take part in this study because you are 18 years or older and are fluent in English. If you decide to take part in the study, you will be asked to answer questionsquestionnaires relating to news content.

This survey will take approximately 2 hours in total and it will consist of four sessions (each ~30 minutes). You may choose to do all four sessions at once or do the four sessions at separate times.

Note:
  • For each session, you must complete them in one setting or your work will be lost.
  • You will only gain 2 course credits through SONA, after completing all four sessions. We do not offer partial credits.

If you have any questions about the study, please email the contact person Sejin Paik at: sejin@bu.edu. Thank you!


Steps for the task:

Step 1:

How does this gun violence-related headline or image make you feel? (choose one of the buttons below and the next step will appear).

Step 2:

What is the intensity of your feeling? (choose one of the options on 5 point scale).

Step 3:

Give us a description (at least 5 words) about WHY you felt the emotion and the intensity of that emotion, based on the wording in the headline or any attributes of the image.
